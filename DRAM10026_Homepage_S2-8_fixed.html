01 — Welcome

This is a course for all of you

Love going to the theatre? Want to learn more about one of Australia's flagship theatre companies? Then you're in the right place. This is a rich and engaging subject, and we're looking forward to some time together — talking theatre, plays, design and acting, and much more besides.

For some of you, going to a live theatre event may be entirely new, while for others you are used to working in independent theatres, or you made theatre at school. It may even be the case that you are already an MTC subscriber. You might be making theatre in your spare time, working as a critic, or a super-fan of the abundance of activity in Melbourne's vibrant independent, fringe and underground theatre and performance scenes. This is a course for all of you. The only thing you need to bring is your life experience and a desire to engage with performance.

It is not 'Rules of theatre', 'Encyclopaedia-Theatre, Australia' or a 'Theatre 1A' subject; instead, we want to turn your attention to the kinds of conversations happening inside Melbourne Theatre Company (MTC) itself: current discussions, the programming of plays, and the dynamic contextual questions and provocations that drive one of the true gems of Australian theatre. Emphasis is placed on you — the audience — and your appreciation of the work of the playwright, director, actor, designer, producer and critic.

Here's the journey we'll take together. It begins, as any love of theatre does, with going — you'll be given tickets to two productions from MTC's subscription season, because no theatre appreciation subject is complete without the live event itself. From there, we'll give you the language to say what you saw: a working vocabulary of theatre terms that lets you move past 'I liked it' and begin to analyse and evaluate how a piece of theatre actually works on you. With that language in hand, you'll start to see the hands behind the work — to recognise what each member of the collaborative team contributes, from the director's interpretation to the designer's world to the actor's craft, and to understand the elements of stagecraft and how a show is built to stand up on a stage. We'll then widen the lens to the forces that shape what we see: the social, cultural and political currents that run through theatre in an Australian context, with MTC's own choices as our worked example. And by the end, the whole thing comes together in the capacity that matters most — the confidence to form a view of your own and defend it: to examine a theatrical event through your own opinion, taste and growing cultural awareness, and to stand behind that judgement as an audience-critic in your own right.

To get you there, the subject combines in-person and Zoom tutorials with weekly module content on Canvas — curated videos, recorded interviews, and resources that build your knowledge of theatre language and MTC's creative processes. Alongside these, a trained actor-tutor will offer practical guidance on speaking in public: how to structure an argument aloud, use your voice with confidence, and hold a room. This isn't a sixth thing to learn so much as the means by which you'll share everything else — it carries you into your Interactive Oral Assessment (IOA), where you'll bring your thinking together in conversation, and it's the kind of skill that stays with you well beyond this subject. Tutorials bring us together at key moments across the semester: we open and close in person at Southbank — including the IOA itself — and meet on Zoom for our two post-show discussions in between.

Our hope is simple: that by the end of the semester your sense of one of this city's significant cultural institutions is richer, and that going to the theatre in Australia's greatest city feels more alive to you than it did before.

Learning Outcomes

What you'll walk away able to do

These are the five things this subject is designed to give you. You don't need any of them to begin — only your life experience and a desire to engage with performance. By the end of the semester, each of these will be yours.

01
Use theatrical terms to analyse and evaluate the methods of artistic and intellectual expression through theatre.
02
Recognise and identify the contributions of key members of the collaborative team of theatre artists who create and perform theatre.
03
Identify and explain some of the basic social, cultural and political influences on theatre in an Australian context.
04
Critically examine theatrical events using opinion, taste and cultural awareness.
05
Identify basic elements of stagecraft and the techniques used for staging theatre.

04 — Assessment

Three ways you'll be assessed

20%
MTC Quiz
Due Friday 21 August · Week 4 · Canvas
A multiple-answer quiz on theatre concepts and terminology from Modules 1–4. Completed online on Canvas.
30%
Research Journal
Due Friday 6 November · Examination period
Four 300-word reflective entries, each responding to a pair of curated videos. Written and/or illustrated. Two draft checkpoints are built into the semester timetable — Checkpoint 1 (Week 5) covers Entries 1 and 2, Checkpoint 2 (Week 10) covers Entries 3 and 4 — and each draft receives peer feedback through FeedbackFruits before you submit the final polished versions.
Your final mark will reflect the quality of your engagement with the draft cycle and your peers' feedback. Students who participate fully in the peer exchange process consistently produce stronger work — and the rubric recognises this.
50%
Interactive Oral Assessment
Week 12 · Wed 21 Oct, 5–7pm · In person · Founders Room, Southbank
A comparative review of both productions, conducted as a group discussion — 10 minutes of focused conversation per student. Supported by a 600-word written overview submitted before your session. All groups assessed on the same evening.
05 — Your Group

Choose your Interactive Oral Assessment group

At the start of semester you'll sign up for one of five IOA groups. This choice shapes your entire experience of the subject — it determines your FeedbackFruits peer group, the students you'll exchange journal drafts and video responses with, and the dedicated tutor who'll guide you through the semester. By the time you sit down for your IOA, you'll already know each other's thinking.
